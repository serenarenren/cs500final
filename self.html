Self: A High School Collection

(2014-2017)

my high school journey documented through self-portraits

Many people take great comfort in putting up a mask to gain acceptance and avoid judgment. We strive for self-love by receiving the respect and confirmation of others. Funnily enough, though we grow to hate the exterior, we grow attached to the love others have expressed for this facade. We grow accustomed to the routine of donning our concealment every morning. Trouble is, as time goes on, the masks we put on break down — they shatter; they crack; they crumble. Revealed beneath is an afraid newborn, a stranger to the world ahead.

My work examines different forms these shields takes, and the process that lies behind their birth and deterioration. I applied my own experience growing up in boarding school, trying on different masks and searching for my true self while surrounded by friends, as the main inspiration. Additionally, my passion for psychology and behavioral science gave me the opportunity to examine adolescent behavior and social cognition with a scientific perspective and in more depth.

This self-portrait reflects where I am in my self-discovery journey. I have learned so much from my experiences, my mistakes, and my peers. I have matured and have a deeper understanding of the world around me. However, there is still so much to learn which is why I left some of the canvas blank to be filled as I continue on my journey.

The barcode-like stripes also address the theme of self worth by connoting the act of labelling oneself.

Course Breakdown

There are three terms at Andover, so we have broken our yearlong course into the three terms below.

Term 1 (Fall)

  1. Hardware, Software, and the Internet
  2. An Introduction to Software Engineering
  3. Java Syntax and Style
  4. Objects and Classes
  5. Data Types, Variables, and Arithmetic
  6. Boolean Expressions and if-else Statements
  7. Algorithms and Iterations

Term 2 (Winter)

  1. Strings
  2. Arrays
  3. Implementing and Using Classes
  4. java.util.ArrayList
  5. Class Hierarchies and Interfaces

Term 3 (Spring)

  1. Algorithms and Recursion
  2. Searching and Sorting

Resources

The following resources can help with students with their learning in CSC500.

  • Java Methods, our book for the course, as well as Be Prepared for the AP Computer Science Exam in Java. Both books are by Maria and Gary Litvin.
  • Coding Bat: Java Challenges
  • Project Euler: Challenging algorithmic problems
  • Stack Overflow: Ask and read answers from virtually any programming question
  • My YouTube Channel: will someday have more videos on it
  • The Java Development Kit (JDK): needed to compile (and run) Java programs
  • Atom or Eclipse to create your code
